Sub: Great Nicobar Rainforest Island Development Megaproject

Most Respectfully,

1. The Great Nicobar Island is a sheer treasure trove of biodiversity, consisting of Biosphere Reserve, Tribal Reserve, National Parks and Wildlife Sanctuaries. This island is perhaps one of the last untouched tropical evergreen rain forests of the world, with endemic fauna and flora much of which is undocumented and unknown to the scientific community. The Island covers an area of 910 sq. of which 865.84 (95.2%) is densely forested, within which is the tribal reserve for the Aboriginal Shompen Tribe comprising 751.40 sq. kms. Two national parks, as part of the biosphere, the Galathea National Park (GNP) spread over 107.10 sq. kms and the Campbell National Park (CNP) 391.75 comprising sq. kms. Hence there is a very strong case to leave this last bastion of nature and biodiversity untouched in its pristine form for future generations. However, the proposed project cuts through the biosphere and apportions 70.6 sq.km into the project area.

2. Great Nicobar Island is inhabited by aboriginal Shompen and Nicobarese, Shompen are approximately 237 and the Nicobarese 1,094 individuals. Though the Nicobarese have joined mainstream, the Shompen Tribes still prefer living in the forests and continue their lives as aboriginal people, living deep within forests, as indigenous tribes world over, in their traditional manner (can be explained in greater detail if required, however Government instrumentalities have extensive knowledge and know the necessity of protecting and preserving the indigenous people in their own lands/forests)

3. At the present moment, 95.2% of the Great Nicobar Island is under pristine forest cover, the proposed project envisages slicing an area of 166 sq. kms. from the total 910 sq. kms which amounts to 18.25% of the Island’s total land mass being diverted for the project. The proposed megaproject envisages 8.5 lakhs trees will be felled and 300 hectares of land reclaimed from dredging up the ocean.

4. A very serious consideration is the role of rainforests being the carbon sinks of the world, and essential to the control of Co2 emissions, which is essential to restrict warming of the planet. In view of the seriousness with which the world is pursuing transitions from fossil fuels to clean energy to cut carbon emissions / global warming, if India proceeds with deforestation on such a massive scale, its will show the country as one of doublespeak, speaking on global forums, and doing the opposite in its country. Such a negative image must be avoided under all circumstances.

5. Further, no studies have been undertaken on what impact such extensive deforestation will have on ocean temperatures; it will certainly lead to a rise in ocean surface temperatures, which will give rise to the El Nino effect, As is well known the El Nino impacts rainfall on the Indian peninsula, which will put an existential threat on the mainland with skewed rainfall patterns and an increased intensity and frequency of extreme/freak weather events.

6. The claimed ‘Holistic’ Development by Govt. of India, MoEFCC fails in as as being neither holistic or all encompassing; it violates all fundamental Environmental Laws Rules and Regulations of the country, preserving the Great Nicobar Biosphere, Protection of National Parks of Great Nicobar, protecting endemic and endangered Wildlife of Great Nicobar, and encroaching on Reserved Forest of indigenous tribes and against the Tribal Policy of Shompen Tribe of Great Nicobar;

7. Hence the entire Mega Project is against nature, indigenous tribes, endangered and endemic flora and fauna and pursues a solely economic agenda, and therefore deserves to be scrapped forthwith and the Great Nicobar Island left in its pristine condition without any further anthropogenic interventions.

Eagerly anticipating an immediate, careful, and most urgent consideration of the above matter from your end.

In Solidarity
An environmentally conscious and concerned citizen.
